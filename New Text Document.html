Inspiration

Soybean meal is a very good protein source for livestock (10–20% of diet). However, its use for young livestock and poultry is limited due to allergenic proteins and anti-nutritional factors, which can cause:

  • Immune irritation
  • Reduced digestion and enzyme activity
  • Weight loss and poor productivity at young stages

How the Technology works

  1. Raw material preparation: Defatted soybean meal is pre-conditioned (adjusted for moisture)
  2. Microbial inoculation: Probiotic strains such as Bacillus subtilis are introduced
  3. Solid-state fermentation: Carried out under controlled conditions
  4. Enzymatic breakdown: Microbes release enzymes (protease, cellulase, pectinase) that:
    • Degrade complex proteins
    • Eliminate anti-nutritional factors
    • Increase levels of small peptides and free amino acids
  5. Drying & milling: The fermented product is dried, sterilized, and ground for feed formulation

Why You Should Choose Our Product

1. Utilizing Low-cost and Readily Available Materials

  • Materials such as soybeans, peanuts, rice bran, okara, and sugarcane bagasse can be directly used as fermentation substrates without dissolving in water.
  • This helps reduce production costs and reuse agricultural by-products effectively.

2. Suitable for Surface-growing Microorganisms

  • Filamentous microorganisms (molds, actinomycetes, yeasts) grow well under moist but non-submerged conditions—like on beans or solid substrates.
  • Semi-solid fermentation allows microbes to produce strong enzymes that efficiently break down proteins, starches, and lipids.

3. Low Water Usage and Easy Contamination Control

  • Because there is no liquid medium:
  • Less risk of unwanted aerobic or anaerobic contamination.
  • Reduced wastewater treatment costs and energy consumption for drying after fermentation.

4. High Enzyme and Metabolite Production Efficiency

  • In semi-solid fermentation, the environment closely mimics natural microbial conditions, enabling higher production of enzymes, amino acids, and vitamins compared to liquid fermentation.
  • Very useful in producing soy sauce, fermented bean curd, miso, tempeh, and industrial enzymes such as protease, amylase, and lipase.

5. Better Flavor and Nutritional Retention

  • Semi-solid fermentation helps:
  • Maintain the basic structure of beans.
  • Develop characteristic aromas through enzyme oxidation and mild Maillard reactions.
  • Increase free amino acids, B vitamins, and functional peptides.

6. Scalable and Environmentally Friendly Process

  • Simple equipment required (trays, containers, racks).
  • Low energy consumption.
  • Suitable for small to medium-scale production or traditional applications integrated with modern technology.

The Nutrient Values of Solid-State Fermentation

Solid-state fermentation (SSF) enhances the nutritional value of raw materials, especially agricultural by-products, through microbial activity. During fermentation, microorganisms such as fungi or bacteria break down complex compounds (like starch, cellulose, and proteins) into simpler, more digestible nutrients.

Here are the key nutrient values from SSF:

  • Increased protein content: Microbes grow on the substrate and add their own biomass, which is rich in protein.
  • Better amino acid balance: Fermentation improves the quality and availability of essential amino acids.
  • Enhanced vitamin content: SSF often increases levels of B-group vitamins and other micronutrients.
  • Improved digestibility: Microbial enzymes degrade fiber and anti-nutritional factors, making nutrients easier to absorb.
  • Production of beneficial bioactive compounds: Such as antioxidants and organic acids that support health.
